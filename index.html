Gender Inequality in the Workplace: Income Inequality

Gender ineqaulity is a major problem in the workplace. Employees who do not feel included in their workplace tend to have worse performance, less sense of belonging, and more mental health problems than those who are. Some employees are given less benefits because of their gender which can be less pay, or even disparity in promotions.

About

Gender inequality seems the most common and ongoing problem. Gender equality is important not individually but is associated with national productivity and economic growth. There is a wage gap between men and women, with women earning 80 cents for every dollar earned by men. Women are also underrepresented in many industries and are often overlooked for promotions and leadership positions. Additionally, women encounter discrimination in the workplace, such as sexual harassment, which can create a hostile work environment. The equality of women in the workplace is low and middle-income countries do not have a fair advantage compared to men. Discrimination also extends to hiring practices, where women may be judged more harshly than men for the same qualifications. All of these issues contribute to gender inequality in the workplace.

Gender equality in the workplace: a global perspective

Click here to open this PDF in a new window


There are many different benefits of diversity in the workplace as it pertains to gender. These benefits include a reduction on sex discrimination and sexual harrassment, as well as the development of products that are not predominantly masculine, and improved team performance, innovation, well being and decision making. These benefits are just a few of many reasons as to why gender diversity in the workplace is critical. This gender diversity can be accomplished in the workplace in ways such as providing screening tools to eliminate gender bias in both the hiring and promotion process, providing multiple extensive diversity training programs to eliminate hidden biases, and by providing access to benefits to improve work-life balance, such as childcare and flexible work schedules.

Income Inequality leads to Physical Inactivity

Click here to open this PDF


This sub-page is meant to represent the statistics which connect women’s economic inequality and decreased physical exercises. Income inequality can cause depression, which is one of the factors that may cause economic disparity and raises the risk of physical inactivity. More individuals live in poverty and lack access to resources like mental health care in areas with higher levels of economic disparity. State health departments interviewed individuals who are part of the civilian non institutionalized population to measure the correlation between physical inactivity and income inequality. Participants were asked several questions in order to determine their physical activity. The analysis in the article resulted in findings that indicated that among US women, income inequality was associated with greater odds of physical inactivity (OR = 1.08,95% CI = 1.05, 1.12) but not with men (OR = 1.01, 95% CI = 0.95, 1.08), meaning that greater inequality was associated with greater odds of being physically inactive among women only (BRFSS). Overall, income has a direct correlation with physical activity, hence why results show more physical inactivity with women than with men.
